SUSPICIOUS TIMING:
Armstrong "discovered" the body exactly 27 minutes after death. Questions: (1) Why did he check Lab 12-A at this specific time? (2) How did he know something was wrong? (3) Why use emergency override instead of calling first?

23:27:18 JST

23:45:12 JST
Armstrong calls Chen. Duration: 12 minutes 33 seconds. Ostensibly "reporting" the death to CFO, but this is 31 minutes after body was discovered and 27 minutes after emergency services were called.
TIMELINE CONCLUSIONS:

1. TANAKA'S DELIVERY: She delivered the poisoned dose at 22:18-22:30 but left BEFORE Yamamoto died. She likely didn't know it was lethal.

2. CHEN-ARMSTRONG COORDINATION: Their 47-minute phone call (22:05-22:52) covered the exact murder window. Both have alibis (on phone together) while maintaining communication throughout execution.

3. ARMSTRONG'S "DISCOVERY": He entered Lab 12-A at precisely 23:14 - only 27 minutes after death. This timing suggests foreknowledge. He then waited 4 minutes before reporting to emergency services.

4. VOLKOV'S ALIBI: She was on Floor 47 (21:15-21:43) but LEFT before the murder. This establishes her presence near the scene but creates distance from the actual execution.

5. FOSTER'S POSITION: On Floor 43 (Medical) during murder timeframe. Four floors below crime scene. Left building shortly after death, before body was discovered.

KEY DEDUCTION: The timeline proves the murder required COORDINATION between multiple parties operating simultaneously from different locations.
