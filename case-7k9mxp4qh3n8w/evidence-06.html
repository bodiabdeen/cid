WITNESS STATEMENTS

EVIDENCE FILE #06 :: CASE #2847-NGT
CONDUCTED BY: Detective Hiroshi Nakamura
DATE: November 24-26, 2084
WITNESSES: Security staff, lab technicians
WITNESS #1: KENJI WATANABE
Senior Security Guard | Interview: Nov 24, 10:15 JST
DETECTIVE: Describe your November 23rd evening shift.
Main security desk, 20:00-04:00. Quiet night. Monitored entry/exit activity.
DETECTIVE: Anything unusual?
No sir. Chen left 23:35, Foster around 22:55. Chief Armstrong was in SOC all evening.
DETECTIVE: You saw Armstrong?
Yes. Delivered paperwork to SOC around 22:00. He was monitoring screens. Saw him again at 23:15, still there, on phone.
WITNESS #2: DR. LISA CHEN
Lab Technician, Floor 43 Medical | Interview: Nov 24, 16:20 JST
DETECTIVE: What do you know about Dr. Yamamoto's gene therapy preparations?
Dr. Foster personally prepared all executive therapy doses. Very secretive about it - only he had access to the formulation records.
DETECTIVE: Did you work on November 21st when Yamamoto's dose was prepared?
Yes. Dr. Foster used the CRISPR synthesis equipment from 08:00-13:12. I was in the adjacent lab. Around 11:30, Dr. Volkov came in and they had a private conversation in the synthesis room for maybe 20 minutes.
DETECTIVE: Could you hear what they discussed?
The door was closed, but voices were raised. I heard Dr. Volkov say something like "you owe me this" and Dr. Foster replied "I know what's at stake." Then silence.
WITNESS #3: HIROSHI NAKAMURA
Delivery Driver, Medical Supplies | Interview: Nov 25, 09:40 JST
DETECTIVE: Tell me about your November 22nd delivery to NGT.
I deliver medical supplies every Friday. November 22nd was normal - dropped off supplies at Medical Division, Floor 43, at 08:45 JST.
DETECTIVE: Did you see anyone from NGT?
Yes, a young woman - petite, black hair, glasses - she signed for the delivery. Very quiet. She seemed nervous, kept checking her phone.
DETECTIVE: Can you identify her?
[Shown photo lineup] Yes, that one. Yuki Tanaka.
DETECTIVE: What time did you leave?
About 09:00. As I was leaving, I saw that same young woman at the medical refrigeration unit. She was placing a small package inside.
WITNESS #4: AKIKO YAMAMOTO
Victim's Wife | Interview: Nov 25, 14:15 JST
DETECTIVE: Mrs. Yamamoto, had your husband seemed worried recently?
[Crying] Yes. The past month he barely slept. He said he'd discovered something terrible at work - trials that were hurting people. He wanted to do the right thing.
DETECTIVE: Did he mention feeling threatened?
On November 20th, he told me he'd made a backup of all his evidence and hidden it somewhere safe. He said "if anything happens to me, someone will find the truth." He was scared, detective. Someone killed my husband to keep him quiet.
